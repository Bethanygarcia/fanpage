Doctors 1-8 played from 1963 to 1989. William Hartnell, Patrick Troughton, Jon Pertwee, Tom Baker, Peter Davison, Colin Baker, Sylvester McCoy, and Paul McGann.

Doctors 9-12 and the war Doctor. Christopher Eccleston, David Tennant, Matt SMith, Peter Capaldi and John Hurt(the War Doctor.)

The thirteenth Doctor. Played by Jodie Whittaker, she is the first major change in terms of casting in Doctor Who history being the first female to ever play the legendary role.

Though the Doctor is known as the Oncoming Storm, the Doctor never seems to be alone. Over the years, companions have come and gone in more ways then one.

With every great hero comes a plethora of villains that will stop at nothing to make sure the Doctor is dead. Throughout the Doctor's thousand years of traveling through time and space, there have been many iconic villains. That would consist of the Master(thought to be the only other Time Lord to survive the Great Time War), the Daleks, the Cybermen, the Judoon, the Weeping Angels, the Ood, the Ice Warriors, the clockwork creatures of the Pompadour, and many more.

The evolution of the TARDIS over the years. It doesn't look like much on the outside because of the damaged chameleon tech which has caused it to remain as the famous blue police box. Along with the famous saying ,"it's bigger on the inside" this wonderous time traveling machine has more than hundreds of corridors and chambers filled with highly advanced Timelord technology from Gallifrey.

The keys to the TARDIS even have their own evolution!

The Sonic Machine factory sure had a variety of products to put out there for the high demand of making life easier... until the Doctor destroyed it. Figures.

As the TARDIS evolved, it has been through some drastic interior renovations. Even the Doctor does some redecorating with some (in)finite spare time.
